Users have a perception of aesthetically pleasing design would work better. Appealing & aesthetically pleasing visual design creates a positive impact which makes the users tolerant of the minor usability issues. Aesthetically pleasing design can obscure usability issues and avoid the detection of problems during usability tests. In most cases, this is a positive thing from your perspective. This effect is a major reason why a good user experience can’t just be a functional UI — designing an interface that’s attractive as well as functional is worth the resources.

Breadcrumbs (or breadcrumb trail) is a secondary navigation system to show the path that the user followed to reach the current screen. Breadcrumbs are intended to supplement other navigational components, such as a global navigation bar that stretches across the top of every page or a local navigation bar that is often found on the left-hand side. Breadcrumbs augment but do not replace those main forms of navigation. Sample Design
